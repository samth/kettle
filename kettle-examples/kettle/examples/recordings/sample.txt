The Zen of Racket
==================

Beautiful is better than ugly.
Explicit is better than implicit.
Simple is better than complex.
Contracts are better than comments.
Flat is better than nested.
Sparse is better than dense.
Readability counts.

Macros are a superpower.
  But with great power comes great responsibility.
  Don't repeat yourself.
  Let the language work for you.

Pattern matching beats nested conditionals.
Structs are your friend.
Immutability is the default (and that's good).
Higher-order functions reduce boilerplate.

There should be one -- and preferably only one -- obvious way.
  Unless you write a macro for it.
In the face of ambiguity, add a contract.
Now is better than never.
Although never is often better than *right* now.

If the implementation is hard to explain, it's a bad idea.
If the implementation is easy to explain, it may be a good idea.
Namespaces are one honking great idea -- let's do more of those!
